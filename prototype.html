We see our conceptual design to be a compact pylon, containing each sensor at appropriate levels to better aid data retrieval. This can be better seen in this diagram (this should be regarded as an early prototype):

Throughout the garden, we will have a set of pylons, each with its own array of sensors, to monitor different parts of it.

V2

We chose to developed a more streamlined and less rigid desgin. We separated each major component (Control Unit and Power Source, Sensors) into different parts in order to create a modular product, thus extending it's target audience:

This design separates the sensor components with a plug-and-play idea in mind. This way, the consumer can choose the sensors he requies with the capabilty of expansion.
The sensors that would be available with this product will record:

  • Soil Moisture
  • Air Temperature and Humidity
  • UV Intesity
  • Position of the sun
  • Sunlight Intesity
  • Motion via Infrared (Replacig the UV sensor)
  • Motion via Ultrasonic Signals (the device can act as a tripwire)
The modules draw power from a the main unit, which can be recharged via USB. An extra module that holds a rechargeable power source is implemented. This one can be recharged with a wireless charger.

V3

This design will remain definitive as our final MVP for the Innovation Fest. Even though it is less ambitious than the V2, completing this build would represent overcoming the technical challenge of programming and assembling our sensor platform, as the same concepts apply for the V2. The desgin is much more compact, with a low profile that would blend easily with it's surroundings.
This design will track and relay information regarding only:

  • Soil Moisture
  • Air Temperature and Humidity
  • UV Intesity
  • Position of the sun
